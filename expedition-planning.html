Small Boat Global Coastal Expedition Planning Guide

This guide outlines the operational framework behind the Blue Rim 5™ Global Sailing Expedition — a disciplined small-boat coastal expedition model supported by guardian vessels and built for real-world seamanship.

The Small Expedition Vessel Model

Blue Rim 5™ operates with small expedition sailboats, typically shallow-draft vessels drawing around 4' or so. These boats offer access, efficiency, and tactical flexibility that larger yachts cannot always provide.

Small vessels reward preparation and seamanship. They demand judgment, weather awareness, and disciplined routing — which is precisely the expedition model being applied.

Shallow Draft Strategy

A vessel drawing around 4' or so gains access to coastal shoals, river mouths, estuaries, and protected anchorages unavailable to deeper-draft cruisers. Draft determines access, and access determines exploration capability.

Historically, shallow-draft vessels succeeded because they were adaptable, not oversized. The same principle guides modern expedition planning.

Guardian Vessel Formation

Small boats operate independently but are supported by larger guardian vessels — both sailing and power — when conditions or logistics warrant. Guardian vessels provide redundancy, weather intelligence, and coordinated response capability.

This layered formation model increases safety without sacrificing small-boat access and agility.

Coastal & Island-Hopping Framework

The expedition model prioritizes coastal progression, island-to-island routing, and controlled daily mileage. This reduces exposure windows while increasing landfall options and cultural engagement opportunities.

Blue Rim 5™ is structured for disciplined coastal exploration, not random bluewater wandering.

Fleet Risk Management

Formation spacing, communication protocols, weather-window discipline, and draft-aware routing form the backbone of the fleet’s operational strategy. Each vessel remains responsible for its own decisions, but reckless independence has no place in expedition sailing.

Expedition Discipline & Decision Making

The sea rewards preparation and punishes complacency. Expedition sailing requires restraint, timing, and humility. Small boats can accomplish extraordinary coastal journeys — when discipline and formation strength are applied correctly.
